From Twinkles in Boredom
to Existential Crisis

A moment of boredom that could teach you more than traditional education.

On a fine lazy semester examination day, I was preparing, zoned out for a while and kept staring at the wall. Light reflected the walls so beautifully. And as I focused more I saw tiny twinkles in the air like little stars glittering. I was so sure I was going insane because of exams and life's existential pressure. But then I wanted to try a different perspective. What if the twinkles were air molecules reflecting light? Interesting. What are the other possible scenarios and how did I miss such pretty scenery in my entire 18 years of life?

Then I started exploring.

The Book That Forced Me to Think

A couple years later I wrote my first solo book, "The Nerdologic Quills" — a 4-dimensional wall breaking narrative where an AI gets struck in the book and undergoes a chaotic experience with the book's soul and takes the reader's help to escape. It explores poems of science, math and comedic chaos while keeping the reader engaged. I researched and understood every dimension to write every poem, which made me realize a few important connections that I might have not encountered otherwise.

Why dimensions matter Physics recognizes at least 11 dimensions in string theory. We experience 3 spatial dimensions and 1 of time. The remaining dimensions are theorized to exist at scales too small to detect directly. Each dimension contains and builds upon the previous one. A being limited to fewer dimensions cannot perceive what exists beyond theirs — the same way a shadow on a wall cannot perceive the object casting it.

The zeroth dimension is a thought. A single point just like the Big Bang or the black hole's centric point. It is where the entire set of dimensions starts and loops back to. The first dimension can be so linear and the "n" number of thoughts in the zeroth dimension can make a series of combinations with different lengths. The second and third dimension and more, all the way to the tenth and eleventh dimension — I researched every one. Not only that but I understood what it would feel like to be a life in that dimension.

The zeroth dimension in physics A point has no length, width, or height. It has only position. The Big Bang is theorized to have originated from exactly this — a zero-dimensional singularity of infinite density and zero volume. Black holes compress matter toward the same kind of point at their centre. Both are moments where all known physics breaks down and something else, something we do not yet have language for, begins.

Then Came the Huge Realization

I can think. Which means I have a thought and a whole set of thoughts. Does that mean my part self is in the zero dimension? So I also live in the higher dimensions? That's why — it clicked. What I do reflects back and affects my higher dimensional self. I felt the entire universe unravel inside me. It made me realize how "I" from each dimension seem to affect my every other self in other dimensions too. And there is a thought that says: your thoughts define you and your life.

So my zeroth dimensional self is defining my life here. And I wondered — am I influencing my higher dimensional self too?

The science that backs this feeling In 2020, astrophysicist Franco Vazza and neuroscientist Alberto Feletti published a study comparing the human brain's neural network with the large-scale structure of the cosmic web. Separated by 27 orders of magnitude in scale, the two networks showed statistically identical organization — same number of nodes, same branching patterns, same distribution of connections. The brain and the universe are not just similar. They are structurally the same. As above, so below — the Upanishads said it thousands of years ago. Physics measured it in 2020.

Yes, I get intuition on what is right. So my higher dimensional self has seen me in different worlds and is giving me the best possible decision to take by looping it back as a thought. This is what they mean by "there is a god within you." It is not just a saying but science we blindly repeat without realizing its depths. These thoughts kept flooding in until I completed all my poems and to this day I keep my actions genuine, honest and peaceful enough — for my supreme self depends on my actions.

Energy is conservative. It does not disappear — it transforms, moves, returns. What you put out does not vanish into nothing. It loops back through a system far larger and more connected than we give it credit for. That is not mythology. That is thermodynamics.
